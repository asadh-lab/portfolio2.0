Dragons

It can’t breath fire or fly, but the megalania(aka Devil Dragon) was the largest terrestrial lizard that ever existed, and is believed to be the ancestor of the modern komodo dragon. The creature could extrete a venomous toxin from its oral glands, and was known to eat large sized mammals and giant marsupials. The megalania could exceed lengths of 26 feet and likely competed with early indigenous settlers of Australia, perhaps contributing to their eventual extinction.

Imugi

The Imugi, also known as the korean dragon, was for years thought to be the stuff of legends. It was thought that such a creature could never exist, that is untilthe python was actually discovered in South America. Now extinct, the Giant titanoboa could grow up to 46 feet long and weighed over a ton. In 2012 a life size model of the creature was displayed in Grand Central station in New York.

Amazonian Warriors

Greek legends and popular superhero Wonder Woman have popularized the notion of the Amazonian in pop culture. Legendary warriors exclusively made up of women. As it turns out such a tribe did exist in Asia minor. Skeletal remains of battle hardened damaged female warriors were discovered in Eurasia, buried with bows, arrows, and other weaponry. Whether you’re a fan of Xena Warrior Princess or an admirer of history this is an extremely interesting find. You can read more about the warriors in this National Geographic article.

Berserkers

Northern European legends tell the tale of legendary warriors who fought in uncontrollable trance-like fury. Their legendary conquests lend credence to the english phrase “Going Berserk.” As it turns out such warriors did in fact exist and fought in battle among vikings. These Juggernauts dominated the battlefield by either working themselves into a frenzy or taking intoxicating drugs before embarking on the battlefield.

Hobbits

In 2004 the skeletal remains of Homo floresiensis were discovered in the Indonesian island of Flores dating back about 18,000 years ago. Each of the nine skeletons was only about 3 feet tall and had skulls about one third the size of normal humans. Sounds like something straight out of a Tolkien novel.

Dracula

It may come as a shock to many, but the infamous vampire was based on an actual person who lived less than 500 years ago. Vlad the impaler or Vlad Dracula was a bloodthirsty romania prince who would torture and kill his enemies by impaling them. He was also known to consume the blood of his victims. Anecdotes of his cruel campaigns and deathly demeanor spread throughout Germany and Italy later inspiring the famous 1897 novel Dracula. Vlad was eventually killed in a military campaign against the Ottoman Empire in January 1477.

Giant Sloths

The most recent discovery on this list was the fact that giant sloths with wolverine claws used to roam America and fought with ancient humans! Fossil evidence uncovered in the sands of New Mexico revealed the particularly curious tale of such creatures. The giant sloth footprints were discovered alongside human footprints suggesting that early humans were closely stalking the beast in search of food. You can read more about the discovery in this Smithsonian article.
